Hunting Locations in Northern Indiana

Public Locations Calendar FAQ

Location 1

Rome City Wetland Conservation Area

The Rome City Wetland Conservation Area includes just over 50 acres of soft marsh and wetlands that can be great for deer hunting. While there are no campsites, hunters are not required to check in, and they allow hunting for both waterfowl and furbearers, which includes deer.

Location 2

Pigeon River Fish and Wildlife Area

The Pigeon River Fish and Wildlife Area is a massive public hunting area that covers over 11,000 acres. There are public rifle and archery ranges, but camping is not allowed. This public hunting area allows hunting for waterfowl and furbearers, including deer.

Location 3

Turkey Creek Wetland Conservation Area

Turkey Creek Wetland Conservation Area is a small, eight acre conservation that is a great spot for ambitious hunters who are willing to get out before most people in order to secure a spot on the land. While camping is not allowed, these eight acres are open to deer, squirrel, and rabbit hunting.

Location 4

Mallard Roost Wetland Conservation Area

The Mallard Roost Wetland Conservation Area is a medium-sized public hunting conservation that spans over 700 acres. Camping is not allowed, but hunting for rabbit, squirrel, deer, and waterfowl is permitted.
